Chicken Fries

About Chicken Fries

Chicken fries are a crispy and flavorful snack made from strips of seasoned chicken that are deep-fried until golden brown. They are popular because of their crunchy texture on the outside and juicy, tender meat on the inside. Chicken fries can be served as an appetizer, evening snack, or side dish with a variety of dipping sauces.

Why People Love Chicken Fries?

One of the best things about chicken fries is their simplicity. They require only a few ingredients and can be prepared quickly at home. The combination of aromatic spices and perfectly cooked chicken creates a delicious dish that is enjoyed by both children and adults. Whether served at parties or as a quick snack, chicken fries are always a crowd favorite.

Ingredients Needed

  • 500 g boneless chicken breast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • Cooking oil for deep frying
  • Tomato ketchup or any dipping sauce (optional)

How to Make Chicken Fries

  1. Wash and clean the chicken breast thoroughly.
  2. Cut the chicken into thin, fry-shaped strips.
  3. In a bowl, add salt, black pepper, chili powder, garlic powder, onion powder, and paprika.
  4. Mix the chicken strips with the spices and let them marinate for 20–30 minutes.
  5. Beat the egg in a separate bowl.
  6. Place flour in one bowl and breadcrumbs in another.
  7. Coat each chicken strip with flour.
  8. Dip the floured chicken strip into the beaten egg.
  9. Roll the strip in breadcrumbs until fully coated.
  10. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  11. Carefully add the coated chicken strips to the hot oil.
  12. Fry until golden brown and crispy, about 4–6 minutes.
  13. Remove and place on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  14. Serve hot with ketchup or your favorite dipping sauce.
