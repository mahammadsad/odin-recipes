Ingredients for authentic Kolkata Biryani

  • Basmati rice (long-grain, aged)
  • Mutton (goat meat) or chicken
  • Potatoes (large, peeled)
  • Eggs (boiled)
  • Onions (thinly sliced)
  • Yogurt (curd)
  • Ginger paste
  • Garlic paste
  • Green chilies
  • Lemon juice
  • Ghee
  • Mustard oil or cooking oil
  • Milk
  • Saffron strands
  • Rose water
  • Kewra water
  • Salt
  • Sugar (a small amount)

Spices

  • Bay leaves
  • Green Cardamom
  • Black cardamom
  • Cloves
  • Cinnamon sticks
  • Mace (Javitri)
  • Nutmeg powder
  • Black peppercons
  • Star anise (optional)

Biryani Masala

  • Coriander powder
  • Cumin powder
  • Red chilli powder
  • Turmeric powder
  • Garam masala powder

Step by step Kolkata Biryani reciper

  1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it for 30 minutes.
  2. Marinate the mutton with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, spices, salt, and lemon juice.
  3. Let the meat marinate for at least 2–4 hours.
  4. Slice the onions and fry them until golden brown (birista).
  5. Boil the eggs and peel them.
  6. Fry the potatoes until light golden and set aside.
  7. Cook the marinated meat until it becomes tender.
  8. Boil water with whole spices and salt.
  9. Add soaked rice and cook until it is about 70–80% done.
  10. Drain the rice and keep it aside.
  11. Soak saffron strands in warm milk.
  12. Grease a heavy-bottomed pot with ghee.
  13. Spread a layer of cooked meat at the bottom.
  14. Place the fried potatoes over the meat.
  15. Add a layer of partially cooked rice.
  16. Sprinkle fried onions over the rice.
  17. Add boiled eggs.
  18. Drizzle saffron milk, ghee, rose water, and kewra water.
  19. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used.
  20. Seal the pot with a tight lid or dough.
  21. Cook on low heat (dum) for 30–45 minutes.
  22. Allow the biryani to rest for 10–15 minutes after cooking.
  23. Gently mix the layers without breaking the rice.
  24. Serve hot with raita, salad, or biryani gravy.
